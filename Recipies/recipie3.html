Kentucky Bourbon Balls

Kentucky Bourbon Balls are a delightful and indulgent confectionery treat that originated in the United States, specifically in the state of Kentucky. These decadent treats are made by combining crushed cookies or cake crumbs with finely chopped nuts, sweetened condensed milk, and, most importantly, Kentucky bourbon.

Kentucky Bourbon Balls contain alcohol, and their consumption should be limited to individuals of legal drinking age.

ingredients

  • 1 cup chopped nuts
  • 5 tablespoons Kentucky bourbon
  • 1 (16 ounce) package confectioners' sugar
  • Â½ cup butter, softened
  • waxed paper
  • 18 ounces semisweet chocolate

Directions for making them

  1. Gather all ingredients
  2. Place nuts in a sealable jar. Pour bourbon over nuts; seal and allow to soak for 8 hours to overnight.
  3. Mix sugar and butter in a medium bowl; fold in soaked nuts with bourbon. Form mixture into 3/4-inch balls and refrigerate for 8 hours to overnight.
  4. Line a baking tray with waxed paper; set aside.
  5. Melt chocolate in the top of a double boiler over just-barely simmering water, stirring frequently and scraping down the sides with a rubber spatula to avoid scorching.
  6. Roll balls in melted chocolate to coat; arrange on the prepared tray. Store in the refrigerator until serving.
